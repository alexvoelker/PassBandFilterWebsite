How To Use
The first step is to generate a picture by pressing the generate button. 

After continuing to the input page. From there you can either use a map to pinpoint there you want to look. Or you can input the longitude and latitude of a certain location. 

Below are the different filters and setting you can control to get the perfect picture for what ever research is being conducted. 

Max Cloud Coverage

The maximum percentage the image is covered by a cloud. Needs to be an integer or decimal number.

Boost Contrast level

A scalar multiplier for image contrast level. Needs to be an integer or decimal number.

Filters

Each filter corresponds to a pixel combination algorithm. They are implemented as follows.

True Color

True representation of colors if a dslr camera was taking an image in space

False Color

Red channel was mixed with infrared helping to reveal planet density

NDWI

Normalized Density Water Index highlights areas of high water content (e.g plants)

SWIR

Short wave infrared highlights areas of water density (darker in areas of ice and light in ares of no water).
